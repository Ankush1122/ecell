Session 1

Impact Lecture Series

Rakesh Ranjan Verma

On 19th July, Board of Innovation & Entrepreneurship at IIT Jodhpur in collaboration with IIC IIT Jodhpur kicked-off it’s ‘Impact Lecture Series’, with the first talk by Mr Rakesh Ranjan Verma, Founder and Director of Elina Energy.

An alumnus of IIM Ranchi and BIT Sindri, Mr Verma had over twenty years of experience in the corporate sector before he initiated his entrepreneurial journey by founding Elina Energy.

Like a Guru to the budding young entrepreneurs, Mr. Verma was more than happy to share his perspective about how to make sense of the transition from a high paying job to a life of relative uncertainty, from a structured setup to an ever-evolving setup.

He explained the source of energy and motivation as also the benefits of making the shift to the world of entrepreneurship. Drawing from his experience with international clients, he highlighted the role of technology in value creation.

He also explained how the nature of the challenge has changed from the time he started the venture to today when he has more than 50 employees working for him. Finally, he highlighted the need to foster an inventive attitude to consistently come up with fresh concepts. The talk was a resounding success, with an overflow of an interactive Q&A session!!

Mr. Verma instilled a new sense of confidence regarding the corporate world and leaving it to pursue your own ventures. The attendees were incredibly delighted to have received all the important points from Mr. Verma’s invaluable experience.

Session 2

Impact Lecture Series

Mr. Gursaurabh Singh

The second talk 19th July brought to us ‘Apni Swadeshi Cycle’, an invention by Mr. Gursaurabh Singh. He is the Founder and creator of Dhruv Vidyut, a company that creates and sells electric conversion kits.

Mr. Gursaurabh had a literature background and was pulled into the world of electricals and electronics because of his interest in long nation- wide travels on a racing motorcycle. project.

He is on the cusp of successfully solving one of the biggest problems of rural India by designing and manufacturing an electric energy conversion device for mechanically driven cycles.

It was inspiring to note how Mr Singh went through numerous design iterations to arrive at his product, all during the depression phase of Covid-19. Mr. GurSaurabh’s invention, ‘Apni swadeshi cycle’, has a portable machine which can be charged through electric source as well as by 20 mins of cycling.

Having stunning features such as water and fire resistance, lightweight mobile phone charging, ability to handle 170 kg load for over 40 kms etc make it a product of high utility. It was exhilarating to note how his invention has gone viral on social media with leaders like Mr Mahindra, Mr Munjal as also NITI Aayog actively engaging with his invention apart from making his posts viral.

There were also talks regarding the possibility of collaborative work with students of IIT J as the venture works with DRDO to certify the product for mass scale usage. Mr Singh’s talk was received incredibly positively by attendees, who were intrigued and curious about the amazing invention. The attendees were inspired to think creatively when trying to solve the problems that truly matter to the society, and solve actual, ground- reality problems in ingenious ways. The session proved to be inspirational, lively, and vibrant!

Session 3

Impact Lecture Series

Ms. Umit Bhatia

On the 30th of July, Board of Innovation & Entrepreneurship at IIT Jodhpur in collaboration with IIC IIT Jodhpur conducted its ‘Impact Lecture Series’, with the first talk of the day by Ms. Umit Bhatia, Vice President - Health, Safety & Sustainable Workplaces Manager of Asia in RBS Business Services Pvt. Ltd.

Not only this, but she is also the one who pioneered the set-up of the ESG, and Occupational Health and Safety Department in Natwest India in 2015. She keeps a keen interest in Climate change and Innovation. She is known in the industry for her leadership skill, she is a great strategic planner and enterprise-wide risk manager. She has a versatile personality and has a diverse career path. She is also a solo artist trained in Hindustani classical music and a member of mentor and mascot Indian film federation.

Ms. Bhatia started by sharing her career journey and how she transitioned into completely different careers. Then she went on to discuss the climate changes happening around and how an individual’s small steps might affect. She also addressed why companies and businesses are compelled to adapt to the Net-Zero initiatives and went on to further explain it as there are some government regulations and mandates for power consumptions. There are supply chain mandates i.e., customer mandates and investor mandates for being e-friendly. The competitive pressure also forces them to adopt the Net-Zero initiative.

Also, she talked about the states adopting the Net-zero initiative. She answered all the questions enthusiastically. From her career journey we get to learn that facing challenges and finding a proper solution on our own is an integral part in the way of success, as she took her challenges as opportunities.

Ms. Bhatia’s talk was truly inspiring, bringing to light for all the young and budding attendees the fact that Climate change requires innovative ways to be combated. Attendees were exhilarated after the talk, and looked forward to implementing Net-zero ideology.

Session 4

Impact Lecture Series

Utkarsh Biradar

The second talk of 30th July was by Mr. Utkarsh Biradar, a Product Designer, Co-Founder & Chief Executive Officer of Flatheads, who has trodden an unusual journey for a designer.

He has gone on to create flagship product companies - After continuous struggles, he has now founded a lifestyle company - Flatheads - with shoes as its flagship product. His company has already revolutionised the way we look at footwear! His journey consists of various twists and turns, starting at IITB, right up to Flatheads and more…

Mr. Utkarsh Biradar started by sharing his experience of how he started his journey when entrepreneurship was not so popular and how he looked for opportunities to push his ideas. He mentioned that his career was half corporate and half in start-ups and he said that the start-up organization is one of the fastest and greatest models to make the world a better place.

From his experience he explained the importance of being able to execute the idea and being thorough in both the macros and micros needed for a successful start-up. His career started with Design Consulting next to Baby product then to Smart shopping app in 2012 and to fabliving (Organic Grocery E-Commerce).

He then started MOMOE payments app in 2013 which provided capital to start his next successful venture of Flatheads in 2020 which was an everyday sneaker E-Commerce. He is an inspiration to fight back failures and march towards success every single time. He also mentioned some of his experiences which showed the importance of changing our thought process frequently to get new ideas, accepting failures, and correcting them every single time to do things with perfection.

This session was really insightful, beneficial and inspirational for sure! The attendees were enthralled after listening to such an inspiring talk, one by an alumnus of the IIT family. They were vested in entering the start-up culture prominent in the country, and in general felt that the talk helped them get bearings about the start-up world.

Women in Entrepreneurship and Mental Health

Ms.Kunika Rathore

We all know about the gender imbalances and discrepancies in the corporate domain and practical world. But that's not going to stay forever. In order to motivate and develop all the excellent young females into futurepreneurs, IIC had invited Ms. Kunika Rathore, to share some invoking ideas on the same. She has been the Founder of the Unknowns Agency, and a mentor at various organizations like FICCI and Delhi University, a software engineer at Hogarth Worldwide and also has been a TEDx Speaker.

She has received various awards which include BTX Top women Executive, India 5000 Women Achiever Awards, Digital Women Award 2021and CEO of the Year 2020.

Her talk about the importance of women in entrepreneurship included specifying how the increasing presence of women as entrepreneurs has led to significant business and economic growth in the country. She also talked about how women owned business enterprises are playing a prominent role in society by generating employment opportunities in the country, bringing in demographic shifts and inspiring the next generation of women founders.

Her dream was to promote the sustainable development of women entrepreneurs for balanced growth in the country. Talking about mental health, she said that it is becoming a huge issue these days. She specified that mental health is more important now than ever before as it impacts every area of our lives.

The importance of good health mentally ripples into everything we do. Maintaining a positive mental health and treating any mental health conditions is crucial to stabilize constructive behaviours, emotions, and thoughts. She also said that some of the physical health problems are directly linked to mental health conditions like heart disease and stress are related, so managing stress might have a positive outcome on heart diseases.

She also patiently answered the questions asked by students which included some tips to maintain mental health. She concluded by reiterating the importance of maintaining mental health which would result in double productivity and also encouraged women to actively participate in all entrepreneurial activities which would promote gender equality too.
